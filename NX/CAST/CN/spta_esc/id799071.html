Modeling flow turbulence

The flow solver uses the Reynolds-Averaged Navier Stokes (RANS) methods to solve for turbulence.

You set the Turbulence Model for your flow analysis on the Solution Details tab of the Solution dialog box.

K-Epsilon (k-ε), K-Omega (k-ω), and Shear Stress Transport —SST are known as two equation models.

In the flow solver, you have the following options to model flow regime:

  • None — Laminar Flow — The flow is not turbulent.

  • Fixed Turbulent Viscosity (FTV) — Turbulent viscosity is assumed to be constant throughout the flow domain. This method converges quickly and is recommended for preliminary results. It is useful in determining general trends and troubleshooting problem areas in the model.

    The model is very sensitive to the specified characteristic turbulence scales.

  • Mixing Length — It converges better than FTV, but it is more computationally intensive. Mixing Length is less accurate than the two equation turbulence models.

  • K-Epsilon (k-ε) — Turbulent viscosity is evaluated at each node by solving two additional conservation equations: one for the turbulence kinetic energy and the other one for the dissipation rate. The total solution time may double or triple. This model requires a finer 3D flow mesh to observe any improvement in turbulence simulation.
