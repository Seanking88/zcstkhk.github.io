In a Guyan reduction, all interior mass is lumped on the boundary DOF.

Correct.

The free/fixed distinction may not be critical to the accuracy of a subsequent dynamic system run solution if the static portion of the dynamic reduction contributes to the local dynamic behavior at the boundary DOFs.

A dynamic reduction combines dynamic and static behavior. The component modes comprise the normal modes, and the constraint modes are equivalent to a static condensation or Guyan reduction.
