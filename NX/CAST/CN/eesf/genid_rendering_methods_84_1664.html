Rendering methods

Option

Description

 Flat

Fastest shading method. Each facet is assigned a uniform color. No shadows or reflections

 Gouraud

Provides smooth interpolated colors. Not quite as fast as Flat shading.

 Phong

Provides smoother highlights than Gouraud shading, but otherwise supports the same level of functionality. Offers lighting effects.

 Improved

Shading with textures material parameters highlights and shadows. Similar quality to photo-realistic but significantly faster. (Available only with High Quality Image.)

Good for images with shiny, polished surfaces.

 Preview

Adds support for transparency without the requirement to anti-alias the image.

Good for images with shiny, polished surfaces.

 Photo-Realistic

Support anti-aliasing and transparency. Images shaded with this mode could take two to three times longer to render than those using the Improved method. (Available only with High Quality Image.)

 Ray Traced

Slower than photo-realistic, but it correctly anti-aliases, or renders, textures. (Available only with High Quality Image.)

Can suffer from pixel dropout where detailed features fall between samples.

 Ray Traced/FFA (Feature Following Anti-aliasing)

Same as Ray Traced , but produces highly anti-aliased images at lower resolutions.

Finds sharp color changes and anti-aliases around features to render as much detail as possible.

 Radiosity

Use this method when physically accurate lighting is required. Indirect lighting in a scene is derived from direct light bouncing off of surfaces before an image is rendered. (Available only with High Quality Image.)

 Hybrid Radiosity

Calculates direct lighting together with radiosity processing for indirect lighting for image rendering. Use this method over Radiosity when precise direct lighting effects are also required. (Available only with High Quality Image.)
