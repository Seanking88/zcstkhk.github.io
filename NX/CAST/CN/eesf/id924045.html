Define a second camera

  1. In the Part Navigator, double-click the Trimetric camera to activate it.

  2. In the graphics window, right-click in the background and choose Orient View→Isometric to orient to a clearer view.

  3. In the Part Navigator, right-click the Cameras node and choose Create.

  4. In the Camera dialog box, in the Name group, in the Camera Name box, type front_lower_right.

  5. In the Target group, click Specify Target Orientation .

  6. In the Point dialog box, in the Output Coordinates group, from the Reference list, select WCS.

  7. In the Point dialog box top banner, click Reset

  8. In the Point dialog box, click OK.

  9. In the Camera dialog box, in the Camera group, click Specify Camera Orientation .

  10. Right-click in the small preview window and choose Fit so you can see all the geometry.

  11. Select the second camera point (2).


  12. In the Custom Zoom group, use the Field of View Angle slider to decrease the field of view until you get a closer view of the teapot.

    In the preview window, you can see that the camera is turned a bit to the right; again, you will fine tune the camera once it has been initially defined.

  13. Click OK.

  14. Rotate the teapot so the orientation is more vertical.

    In the Part Navigator, the camera is now out-of-date because you rotated it after defining it.

  15. In the Part Navigator, right-click on front_lower_right and choose Capture View.
