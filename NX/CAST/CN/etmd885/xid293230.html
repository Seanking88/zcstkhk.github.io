Edit the pitch of the helix using law curves

By editing the pitch of the helix you can explore different design configurations. In this activity, you will use pre-defined curves that have curvature parameters that define the pitch of the helix.

The law curves are on a separate layer.

  1. On the Utility toolbar, click Layer Settings .

  2. In the Layer Settings dialog box, in the Layers group, in the Name column, select the 100 check box, then click Close.

    Each curve can be used as a law curve to control the radii of the helix.

    1. Law curve 1

    2. Law curve 2

  3. In the Part Navigator, double-click Helix.

  4. In the Helix dialog box, in the Size group, set Law Type to By Law Curve.

  5. Select curve 1 as the law curve.

  6. Click OK.

  7. In the Part Navigator, double-click Helix.

  8. In the Helix dialog box, in the Size group, click Select Law Curve (1) and then press Shift and select the law curve to deselect it.

  9. Select curve 2 as the law curve.

  10. Click OK.
