A new feature automatically becomes a member of the active feature group

  1. Choose Preferences→Modeling.

  2. Click the General tab.

  3. At the bottom of the dialog select the Allow Automatic Reordering of Feature Groups check box.

  4. Click OK.

  5. In the Part Navigator right-click on the feature group tool_body_left.

  6. Choose Active to make this the active feature group.

    Notice that the icon changes color to indicate it is the active feature group. Any new features will automatically become members of this group.

  7. On the Feature toolbar, click Edge Blend .

  8. At the top of the dialog box, click Reset .

  9. In the graphics window select the two edges shown.

  10. In the Radius 1 box, type 6 and press Enter.

  11. Click OK.

    Notice that the new edge blend, Edge Blend (48) is automatically added to the active feature group. Also, the active feature group, Feature Group (48) “tool_body_left” has been automatically reordered to Feature Group (49) “tool_body_left”.

  12. In the Part Navigator, right-click on the feature group tool_body_left and select Active again to make it inactive.

    Or you can right-click in the background of the Part Navigator and choose Make No Group Active.

  13. Remember to change your Customer Defaults back to their original settings before closing the part.

  14. Close the part without saving.
