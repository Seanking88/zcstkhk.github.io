Predefined constraints

To facilitate modeling common structural boundary conditions, Design Simulation provides a variety of predefined constraint types. Some examples include:

  • A Fixed Constraint that fixes all translational and rotational degrees of freedom.

  • A Pinned Constraint that creates a cylindrical coordinate system at the center of a cylindrical face. Tangential translation (DOF2) is free; all other DOFs are fixed.

  • A Symmetric Constraint that fixes translation normal to the plane of symmetry and fixes rotation about axes lying in the plane of symmetry.

For a complete description of predefined structural constraints in Design Simulation, see Structural Constraints in the Design Simulation online Help.
