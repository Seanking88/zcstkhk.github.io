Hand-calculating strain and displacement

You can apply an axial pressure load to a simple bar and then remove it to predict permanent strain due to plasticity. The applied pressure load will induce a stress/strain condition beyond the elastic limit.

The following graph shows the stress/strain curve for the material applied to the bar. Stress (on the vertical axis) is plotted against strain (on the horizontal axis). The red line shows plastic strain; the green line shows elastic strain. When removing the load, the stress/strain curve returns to the zero-stress condition along a path parallel to the elastic line.

Calculating plastic strain

The plastic strain for this simple one dimensional problem is calculated as follows:

Total Strain = Plastic Strain + Elastic Strain

Total strain is determined by the stress level and the stress/strain data for the material. In this case, the applied pressure and stress are identical.

As shown in the stress/strain curve, when Stress = 46250 psi, Total Strain = 0.015.

Elastic Strain is calculated as follows:

Elastic Strain = Stress / Elastic Modulus

Substituting the known stress when the load is applied and the elastic modulus gives:

Elastic Strain = 46250/26.9E6 = 0.00172

To solve for plastic strain:

Plastic Strain = Total Strain - Elastic Strain

Substituting known values gives:

Plastic Strain = 0.01500 - 0.00172 = 0.01328

Calculating residual displacement

Engineering Strain = Change in Length / Original Length

Therefore:

Residual Displacement = Plastic Strain x Original Length

Residual Displacement = 0.01328 x 5 in = 0.06640 in.
