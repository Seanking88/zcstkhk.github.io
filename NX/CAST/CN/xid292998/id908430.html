Create a spatially varying pressure

Apply a spatially varying pressure to the portion of the sheave in contact with the belt. The mathematical expression representing the pressure distribution is written in terms of the cylindrical coordinate system created in the previous step.

    50*cos(ug_var("theta"))

  •  Accept Edit

  • The expression is written in dimensionless form. The software automatically interprets the numerical results of the expression as having the units of the dependent variable. In this case, the dependent variable is pressure and the units are lbf/in^2(psi).

    Alternatively, to write the expression in dimensional form, you must ensure the units resulting from evaluating the expression are identical to the units of the dependent variable. Because the cosine of theta is dimensionless, you can write the above expression in dimensional form as:

    Expressions (box under the table)

    50[lbf/in^2(psi)]*cos(ug_var("theta"))
